# benham_top

> spinning black and white disk. specific arc patterns. no color in the source. stare at it and color emerges. fechner colors. the brain's temporal processing turns luminance flicker into hue.

**Archetype:** SURFACE. **Distinct from** `rotating_snakes` (color-phase motion, not temporal color) and `simultaneous_contrast` (spatial color interaction, not temporal).

## Adjacency
- `rotating_snakes` — motion illusion cousin, different mechanism
- `afterimage_painter` — temporal color adaptation, different timescale
- `simultaneous_contrast` — spatial color interaction

## Visual Targets
- Black disk with white arc sectors
- Arc pattern: 3, 4, or 6 sectors with specific angular widths
- Rotation speed: 2–10 Hz (critical for color emergence)
- Subjective color: typically blue, green, yellow, red depending on sector width
- Parameter space: arc count × arc width × rotation speed × disk size
- Strobe mode: variable flicker frequency for color tuning

## Technical Notes
- Fechner colors: explained by temporal frequency tuning of color-opponent channels
- L/M cone imbalance at temporal flicker creates hue sensation
- Arc width ~ 15°–45° optimal for subjective color
- No actual color in the stimulus — all in the neural processing
